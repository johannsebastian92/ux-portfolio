Problem

Whenever you open up the news, there are countless articles on the difficulties of everyday people with their finances. In 2018,the outstanding student loan debt totals 1.5 trillion dollars. As of 2019, the average US household has an estimated credit card balance of $6,849. Yet in a recent poll done by debt.com, results found that 1 out of 3 Americans don’t use a budget. Even though it is one of the most effective ways to manage one's finances. The following case study seeks to understand the deeper issues people have with money in order to create a product that can assist in this crisis.

Solution

Worth is a mobile budgeting application that offers users the ability to take control of their finances. The final product offers users a tool that allows them to budget, track expenses, and create goals.

Key Insights

The survey revealed that participants in some form or another struggle with money. Although they have goals, the charts above show participants have a modest outlook of their current financial standing as well as their abilities to manage money.


The one underlying issue that all these problems encompass is that participants use ineffective budgeting methods as approximately 40% of participants use a personal system to manage their finances but still struggle with issues that prevent them from achieving their financial goals.

Competitive Analysis

After completing the user survey and identifying the main issues users have with their finances. I conducted a competitive analysis which revealed the key strengths and opportunities as well as the threats and weaknesses of the market.


Weaknesses

Price. Complicated features. Impersonal tone.


Strengths

Understandable and attractive user interface. Regardless of the product, users find these products useful and effective.

Logo

The logo is meant to represent peace of mind when it comes to one’s finances. The logo should be white in dark backgrounds and Dark Gunmetal on lighter backgrounds.

Typography

Playfair Display is well-suited for headlines and titles with a contemporary feeling that will give designs the sophistication they need. Product Sans is simple and approachable allowing users to easily process content.


Color Palette

Dark Gunmetal and Green Blue is serious and conveys the importance of finances and a person’s goals. Radical Red, Mountain Meadow, and Deep Saffron gives the brand a friendly and inviting feeling.


Style Guide

Feedback

With all the research, information architecture, and branding completed, I created my first set of mockups and asked other Designers for feedback.


Key Insights

Multiple Designers commented that forcing the user to immediately set up the application after creating an account is too lengthy and will cause dropoff. As a result, I removed the onboarding process and added empty states to pages in the app that needs to be set up. From there the user will be able to set up each feature as they see fit.

Attaining Feedback

Pre-Test: Focused on demographics and the participants relationship with money


Post-Task: Noted the completion task time, asked users to rate the task difficulty, as well as their motivations for the path they took.


Post-Test: Focused on the user’s overall experience with the product, what they liked, and what didn’t.

Results

Key Insights

Participants found completing each task easy as there was no score under 4.3 out of 5. In order to confirm the user’s rating, I compared the task time with the rating given. If the task time matches the rating, I deemed that score valid. I also looked over the post-test interviews to find areas that can be improved. The comment below gave me the most insight on how to improve the product.


"Did you find anything difficult or caused you frustration with the product?"

“Although the words are easy to understand. I feel like some things can be described better by using different words or icons. Like the home page and buttons. When you get to the actual home section it’s more like a budget page. But you already have a budget page too. So that can be kind of confusing… The words in the goals section can be said better too.”

Conclusion

Creating a product that helps people get control of their finances has always been an aspiration for me. And to be able to speak to users - understand their concerns, goals, and what drove them and use that research to construct Worth was largely a success.


What worked?

Worth answers the major issue that arose during the research and discovery phase; which was users need a system that assists them in getting their finances in order. Worth provides users a simple and effective budget method, the ability to track goals, as well as real-time transactions. All of which help them achieve financial peace of mind.


If I had more time…

Creating goals when it comes to money can be complicated. There are so many options and variables to consider. The interest rate, fees, and mortgage are just some things to consider. If I had more time, I would have developed the goals feature of this application by continuing my research.
