My Journey Towards Design

From the very beginning, I wanted my life to be about helping others. The experience of being born in the Philippines and being raised in Guam along with seeing the struggles of others gave me such a unique perspective on life. These experiences developed a sense of empathy for people that I wanted to harness. Little did I know that these encounters were just the beginning of my journey towards design. Throughout my education at San Francisco State University and experience in the healthcare field, I learned a couple of things that inform my approach to UX design.


First, I became a scientist. Research is essential to the success of any product. And being in an undergraduate program so research-based taught me how to read complex studies and form fact-based assumptions that can be tested for data. The data would then be analyzed and presented while receiving feedback from professionals in the healthcare field.


Second, I learned how to communicate with people to understand their goals, needs, motivations, and issues and used those conversations to create great experiences for my clients. These encounters developed my emotional intelligence which is essential to UX and understanding users in order to create a product fitting of their needs.


Over time, I was pulled more and more towards an innovative career path where I could incorporate my humanistic nature and decided to enroll in Bloc’s Designer program. UX design is the perfect marriage for my curiosity in solving problems and my desire to help others. Through the curriculum and my mentor’s guidance, I was able to refocus my skills towards design.
